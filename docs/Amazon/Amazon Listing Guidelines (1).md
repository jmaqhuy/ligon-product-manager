# Product Title Requirements and Guidelines

The product title or product name (the **Item name** field on the **List your products** page) is the first piece of information that customers see when they search for a product on Amazon. Titles must clearly and concisely describe key product information. Clear titles help customers to identify, differentiate, and compare products, which simplifies their decision-making process and increases the likelihood of sale.

---

## Product title requirements

To list a product for the first time, your title must meet the requirements described below:

| Requirement type | Details |
|---|---|
| **Character limit** | Titles must not exceed 75 characters, including spaces. |
| **Promotional content** | Titles must not contain promotional phrases, such as "free shipping" or "100% quality guaranteed." |
| **Special characters** | Titles must not use the following special characters: `!`, `$`, `?`, `_`, `{`, `}`, `^`, `¬`, `¦`. Other special characters, such as `~`, `#`, `<`, `>`, and `*`, are allowed only in specific contexts. For example, you may use these symbols as product identifiers ("Style #4301") or measurements ("<10 lb"). Decorative usage of special characters is not allowed. For example, the title "Paradise Towel Wear Co. Beach Coverup << Size Kids XXS >>" is non-compliant because of the excessive use of symbols around the size. |
| **Content requirements** | Titles must contain the minimum amount of information that can be used to clearly describe the product, such as "Amazon Essentials Dress," "Columbia Hiking Boots," or "Sony Headphones." Do not include restricted phrases, such as "FSA/HSA eligible." |
| **Word repetition** | Titles must not contain the same word more than twice. For example, "Baby Boy Outfits Baby Boy fall Winter Clothes Baby Boy Long Sleeve Suspender Outfit Sets" is a non-compliant title. Prepositions, articles, and conjunctions may be used more than twice in a title. However, brand names are also subject to the two-instance limit. If part of a brand name appears in a different context (for example, "Old Navy and Navy Blue"), it is not considered a duplicate. |

## Best practices

Creating high-quality product titles is important to help customers discover your products and can increase conversions. Customers quickly scan search results, so ensuring that your title captures the most important product information is key to making your listing discoverable. Additionally, long or cluttered titles can be hard to read. The ideal title should concisely provide key information about the physical product, without excessive detail or non-essential information.

If the title length is insufficient to include all details of your product, a new field called **Item highlights** has been introduced that you can use to add additional descriptive information (refer to the FAQ below for more details).

To provide the best possible customer experience, follow these guidelines:

| Guideline | Compliant example | Non-compliant example | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Length:** Keep titles concise (maximum 75 characters) | "Amazon Essentials Women's Fleece Jacket, Black, Medium" | "Amazon Essentials Super Comfortable Women's Winter Fleece Jacket with Pockets and Zipper Closure Perfect for Cold Weather and Outdoor Activities, Available in Black Color, Size Medium for Women" | 75 or fewer characters is required because mobile screens truncate long titles. |
| **Redundancy:** Avoid repeating words or information | "Levi's Men's 501 Original Fit Jeans" | "Levi's Men's Jeans Men's 501 Original Fit Men's Denim Jeans" | Non-compliant example repeats "Men's" and "Jeans" unnecessarily. |
| **Special characters:** Avoid excessive or decorative use of symbols. Punctuation should be used where needed | "Brother Printer, HL-L2350DW Wireless Monochrome" | "Brother Printer★HL-L2350DW★Wireless Monochrome★BEST SELLER★" | Use only standard letters and numbers. Don't use non-language ASCII characters such as Æ, Š, Œ, Ÿ, or Ž. Titles can include necessary punctuation, like hyphens (-), forward slashes (/), commas (,), ampersands (&), and periods (.). |
| **Information order:** Brand name, flavor or style, product type, key attribute, color, size or pack count, model number | "Amazon Fresh Decaf Colombia Whole Bean Coffee, Medium Roast, 12 Ounce" | "Medium Roast Decaf Coffee 12oz Pack of 3 Colombia Whole Bean Amazon Fresh" | Order the words to reflect the most important product information upfront. |
| **Parent/child variations:** Include size and color in child ASINs only and not in the title of the parent ASIN | Parent: "Amazon Essentials T-Shirt" / Child: "Amazon Essentials T-Shirt, White, Medium" | Parent: "Amazon Essentials T-Shirt, Available in Multiple Colors and Sizes" / Child: Same as parent | The detail page displays the title of the parent ASIN, and the title of the child ASIN will only appear once the ASIN is added to the customer's cart. The child ASIN title will not appear on the product detail page. |
| **Numbers:** Use numerals instead of spelling out numbers | "2-Pack Cotton Towels, 24 x 48 inches" | "Two-Pack Cotton Towels, Twenty-Four x Forty-Eight inches" | Use numerals: "2" instead of "two". |
| **Subjective commentary:** Don't use subjective phrases | "Sony WH-1000XM4 Wireless Noise Canceling Headphones" | "Sony WH-1000XM4 HOT ITEM Wireless Noise Canceling Headphones - BEST SELLER!!!" | Subjective commentary examples are "Hot Item" or "Best seller". |
| **Capitalization:** Don't use all caps | "Nike Air Running Shoes with Cushioned Sole" | "NIKE AIR RUNNING SHOES" or "nike air running shoes" | Capitalize the first letter of each word, except for prepositions (in, on, over, with), conjunctions (and, or, for), or articles (the, a, an). |
| **Abbreviations:** Measurements can be abbreviated | "Samsung 55-inch 4K Smart TV, 55 in, 2023 Model" | "Samsung Fifty-Five inch 4K Smart Television, Fifty-Five inches, 2023 Model" | Measurement abbreviation examples: "cm", "oz", "in", and "kg". |

## Frequently asked questions

**How can I add more descriptive information about my product if the required title length is only 75 characters?**

When you update your title to the 75-character limit, you will be able to add item highlights. Item highlights provide an additional 125 characters for product details like materials or recommended use cases. These should be written as comma-separated phrases, instead of full sentences.

For example: Instead of a long title such as "Fast Charger Adapter, PPS Supported, Compact Charger for models like MacBook Air/Macbook Pro/iPhone 14/iPhone 13/Galaxy S22/iPad Pro/Pixel and More, Cable Not Included", add the key product details such as "USB-C, PPS Support, Cable not included" to the item highlights for a better customer viewing experience.

---

**How will item highlights be visible to customers along with titles?**

Item highlights will be visible below titles in search results and on product detail pages.

---

**What should I do if my product doesn't have a brand name?**

For products without a brand name, enter "generic" in the "Brand name" field. Do not enter a brand name in the title.

# Product Descriptions  
In addition to the manufacturer's description, you can add your own description of your products. Give concise, honest, and friendly overview of its uses and where it fits in its category. Discuss the features and benefits of the product and focus on your product's unique properties. Don't mention competitors. Highlight the best applications for the product. If the product has limitations, you can say so and upsell. Check spelling and grammar before submitting. The Product Description is a text-only field with a limited number of characters that varies by product category.

## Policies for Writing Listings  
* Don't use HTML, JavaScript, or other types of code in your product detail pages. As a special case, you can use line breaks </br> in the description.  
* None of the following are allowed in product detail page titles, descriptions, bullet points, or images:  
    * Phone numbers, addresses, email addresses, or website URLs  
    * Details of availability, price, or condition  
    * Links to other websites for placing orders, or alternative shipping offers, such as free shipping  
    * Requests for positive customer reviews  
    * Adverts, promotional material, or watermarks on images, photos, or videos  
    * Brand-specific information, such as a brand's intellectual property, on a "generic" product listing  
    * Restricted phrases such as "FSA/HSA eligible"  
  
## Policies for Adding Detail Pages  
* You cannot add details which infringe upon the IP rights of other parties or individuals.  
* You must not use false product identification information in product detail pages. This includes UPC codes and publication dates.  
* You must not use product detail pages to cross-merchandise or cross-promote a product.  
  
# Bullet Points  
Bullet points are concise statements that highlight key product features and benefits in order to help customers make informed purchasing decisions. Well-crafted bullet points can increase product discoverability in search results and can improve conversion rates by clearly communicating essential product information. Bullet points help customers quickly understand if the product is right for them.  
  
## Bullet Point Guidelines  
Writing guidelines for high-quality bullet points:  

| Component | Best Practices |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Capitalization | Begin with a capital letter. |
| Bullet point count | Include at least five bullet points. |
| Character length | 150-255 characters per bullet point. |
| Formatting | Format as a sentence fragment, and don't use end punctuation. Use semicolons to separate phrases within a single bullet point. |
| Numbers | Write numbers one to nine in full, excluding names, model numbers, and measurements. |
| Measurements | Include a space between digit and unit (example: "60 ml"). |
| Structure | Add a header with a colon followed by a description (example: "Cotton fabric: Made from 100% cotton"). |
| Content focus | Highlight product features, benefits, and how the product meets customer needs. Avoid brand marketing stories. |
| Content consistency | Maintain data consistency across product variants. Minimize duplication with other product attributes such as title, product description, or product overview. |
| Claims | Avoid subjective, performance, or comparative claims unless they're verifiable on packaging. Avoid unsubstantiated award claims or consumer survey results. |
| References | Do not divert or refer to other products not included under the ASIN. Do not compare your product to competitor brands. |
